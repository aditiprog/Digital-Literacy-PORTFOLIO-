# Stay Safe Online: Prevention Checklist for Indian College Students 



A practical checklist with 10 actionable tips tailored for handling UPI payments, shared Wi-Fi, and online assignments.

## Checklist

- **Use strong, unique passwords**: Create passwords with 12+ characters mixing letters, numbers, and symbols (e.g., "VITBhopal2026!Chem"). Use a password manager app like LastPass or Bitwarden, and enable two-factor authentication (2FA) everywhere—especially Google, college email, and banking apps.
- **Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive tasks**: College hotspots are hack-prone; use mobile data for logins, UPI transfers, or accessing grades/portals. Install a VPN like ExpressVPN or free ProtonVPN when on shared networks.
- **Verify links and apps before clicking**: Hover over suspicious texts/emails (e.g., "Your scholarship approved!") to check URLs. Download apps only from Google Play Store or official sites—fake UPI apps steal PINs.
- **Secure your UPI transactions**: Never share UPI PIN or approve payments via SMS/calls. Set UPI limits (e.g., ₹5,000/day) in PhonePe/GPay apps, and use virtual payment addresses (VPA) instead of phone numbers for peer transfers. *(Financial/UPI safety)*
- **Enable biometric locks on financial apps**: Use fingerprint/face ID on UPI apps like Google Pay or PhonePe. Avoid screenshotting balances/statements—scammers target shared devices in hostels. *(Financial/UPI safety)*
- **Keep software updated**: Regularly update your phone OS, apps, and antivirus (e.g., Avast or Malwarebytes free version) to patch vulnerabilities exploited in Indian phishing waves.
- **Beware of job/internship scams**: Ignore WhatsApp/Telegram offers for "easy data entry" jobs asking for registration fees via UPI—common trap for students. Cross-check on college career portals or LinkedIn.
- **Don't overshare on social media**: Set Instagram/Facebook profiles to private; avoid posting room numbers, hostel details, or live locations—stalkers use this for physical threats.
- **Scan for malware regularly**: Use built-in Google Play Protect or apps like Quick Heal to scan downloads, especially pirated study PDFs or cracked software popular among students.
- **Report incidents immediately**: For cyber fraud, scams, or harassment, visit [National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in)](https://cybercrime.gov.in) to file a complaint, or call the helpline **1930** (24/7 toll-free).
