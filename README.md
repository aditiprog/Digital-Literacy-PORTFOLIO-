# Digital-Literacy-PORTFOLIO-
# Digital Literacy & Professional Presence Project

## 📌 Project Overview
This project introduces the digital world and guides students in:
- Navigating online platforms
- Building a personal portfolio
- Establishing a professional presence on the internet
- Creating strong personal branding

No prior coding knowledge is required. All tools used are **free** and **browser-based**.

---

## 🌐 Key Topics
- **Digital Literacy**: Understanding what it means and why it matters
- **Useful Digital Tools for Students**
- **Safe Internet Practices**
- **Professional Online Presence**
- **Email Etiquette**
- **Creative Texts & Fonts**: Neon, glitch, 3D, handwritten, vintage styles
- **Presentation Tools**: Prezi, PowerPoint, Canva, Miro, Google Slides (SlidesCarnival templates)

Create foundational accounts across key platforms:

## 📝 Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz
Create a **Google Form** with exactly 5 questions:
- At least **1 multiple choice question**
- At least **1 short answer question**
- Focus: *Communicating professionally online*

---

## 📧 Email Etiquette
Emails must include:
- Clear **subject line**
- Professional **greeting**
- Well-structured **body**
- Proper **sign-off**

Save drafts as **plain text** or **Word/PDF**.

---

## 📱 Responsible Social Media Use
Checklist for college students:

### ✅ Do’s
- Keep profiles professional  
- Share authentic content  
- Respect privacy of others  
- Use strong passwords  
- Verify information before sharing  

### ❌ Don’ts
- Overshare personal details  
- Post offensive or harmful content  
- Engage in online arguments  
- Share unverified news  
- Ignore privacy settings  

---

## ⚠️ Online Threat Awareness
Research a cybercrime scenario (e.g., phishing, UPI fraud, identity theft, cyberbullying, ransomware).

Document:
1. **What the crime is**
2. **How it typically happens (step-by-step)**
3. **Who is usually targeted**
4. **Consequences**

---

## 🛡 Stay Safe Online Checklist
At least 8 actionable prevention tips:
- Use strong, unique passwords  
- Enable two-factor authentication  
- Avoid clicking suspicious links  
- Verify sender emails before responding  
- Keep software updated  
- Use secure payment gateways  
- Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive transactions  
- Report suspicious activity immediately  

---

## 📊 Evaluation Criteria
- **Emails**: Subject line quality, tone, structure, salutations, sign-offs  
- **Quiz**: Relevance and clarity of questions  
- **Portfolio**: Professionalism and completeness  
- **Checklist & Awareness Resource**: Practicality and accuracy
